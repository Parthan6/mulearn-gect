HOW IT BEGAN

GEC THRISSUR · FIRST EXECOM · 2024

Government Engineering College, Thrissur, had always produced engineers of rare capability. But capability without direction is a library with no readers.

In 2024, eleven students looked at their campus and saw something others had accepted as normal: potential everywhere, and no structure to channel it. There was no μLearn chapter here. No council approval. No events. No community. Nothing.

So they built it.

The first team did not inherit a stage. They built one.

They sat with college administration until the council was approved. They ran the first online events when the audience was uncertain. They brought the All India Comic Association to campus for a storytelling workshop that nobody expected. They stood in the halls of Permute — μLearn's national event — and put GEC Thrissur on the map for the first time.

They ran Figma workshops. SolidEdge workshops. They planted the seeds of interest groups — small gatherings of students who wanted to go deeper — not knowing those seeds would one day become full Communities of Practice.

When the first execom left, they left behind something more valuable than any event or workshop.

They left a foundation. A culture. A proof that it could be done.

The second team took that foundation and built higher — μLearn Mondays, Communities of Practice, institutional recognition. All of it grew from what the first eleven made possible.

This website is for those eleven. The ones who started with nothing and made everything that came after possible.

WHAT GREW AFTER US

TO THE SECOND EXECOM — WITH PRIDE

We planted seeds before we left.

The second execom took those seeds and built things we only dreamed of.

μLearn Monday — a bi-monthly speaker series bringing industry leaders, designers, artists, and entrepreneurs to this campus. The kind of exposure we always wanted to create.

Communities of Practice — the interest groups we started, grown into full cohort-based bootcamps: AI/ML, UI/UX, Blockchain. Students going deep, not just attending.

Institutional recognition. Accreditation. A formal roadmap submitted to the college.

We don't take credit for what they built. But we will quietly take credit for making it possible.

To the second execom: you made us proud.
